Typical food and where to try it

Spain is a country with a lot of gastronomic variety in its different communities. In the case of Salamanca, this city stands out for its so-called “tapas”. Tapas are an appetizer of Spanish cuisine served as an accompaniment to drinks such as wine and beer. It is normally consumed in bars, but that does not mean that it cannot be enjoyed after food, before desserts or during homemade lunches.

Some typical tapas of the city could be the "patatas meneas", which are potatoes mashed with paprika and often accompanied with torreznos or picos, also there is the calderillo, a very common stew in the community. In addition, there are other dishes such as hornazo, which is an patty that is filled raw with eggs, chorizo and ham and baked in the oven, or Ledesma donuts, which are typical sweets from this town.

Night-life

Revels in Salamanca

That Salamanca is known as one of the best university cities in Spain is no exaggeration.
University students, who largely come from other provinces or even countries, enjoy a great nightlife thanks to the number of pubs, bars and clubs there are. As in many provinces, the most common thing is to go out “to the bars” first and then go to the clubs, where in several you do not pay entry. The distance between them is no more than 1 minute, which is why it is very common to see people outside the clubs or going to others. Although the drinks are usually expensive, around €8, other cities like Madrid or Barcelona far surpass them in price. The most trend clubs among students are:
- Fletcher, Pl. Reina, 6, 37001 Salamanca.
- Moma, Pl. del Corrillo, 19, 37002 Salamanca. 677 35 29 00
- Camelot, Calle Bordadores, 3, 37002 Salamanca.
- Berlin, C. Correhuela, 9, 37001 Salamanca. 923 21 98 30

The convenience of being a city of about 39.34 km² means that everything is close, so the most common thing is to get around on foot. On many occasions it is not necessary to have to take the car and not be able to drink because of it, since unless you are staying on the outskirts of the city, using the car will only be a hindrance.
But the party in Salamanca is not limited to the night. They also have a large number of capeas and activities throughout the day, some from 12 in the morning until 10 at night. In addition to the well-known Salamanca fair and its patron saint festivities.

What to visit?

Salamanca is one of the cities with the most history in Spain, dating back to the Celtic era about 2,700 years ago. This city has a great historical architectural heritage with which it has been awarded several distinctions, for example, it was declared a World Heritage by the UNESCO in 1988 and in 2002 European Capital of Culture together with the city of Brujas.

This city has been preserved with great impetus which entails the significant importance of its large number of monuments in Gothic, Romanesque and Baroque style, although what most identifies Salamanca is the plateresque, and its University. The Old City of Salamanca stands out for preserving a large number of monuments of significant importance in the Romanesque, Gothic, Arabic, Renaissance and Baroque styles, although what most identifies the city is the Plateresque (similarity between the type of detailed decoration used in the buildings of the time and the work of the silversmiths, architectural style of the first Hispanic Renaissance) and its University.

You cannot visit Salamanca without taking walks through its old and preserved streets or seeing its monuments, which is why we leave you a list of the places to visit. Furthermore, thanks to the small size of the city and the limitations of traffic circulation, the city is perfect to discover on foot.

- The Plaza Mayor of Salamanca is one of the main tourist attractions as it is one of the most beautiful squares in Spain. It was built in the mid-18th century and is one of the most representative examples of the baroque in Spain.
‍
- Plaza del Corrillo, a small square in which if you look at the top of its columns you will see that they are decorated with eggs, since in ancient times Eggs were sold here.
‍
- The Calisto and Melibea garden is one of the special corners of Salamanca, so called because Fernando Rojas chose this place for the meetings of the protagonists of his work in "La Celestina".
‍
- The House of the Shells or the which is currently the Public Library of Salamanca, was built in the 15th century as a mansion. On its curious façade you can see more than 300 shells and the Maldonado coat of arms, with five fleurs-de-lis, the symbol of the owners of the house.
‍
- In front of the Casa de las Conchas is the Clerecía, which is a beautiful building Erected by the Jesuits as a seminary in the 18th century, although it is currently the headquarters of the Pontifical University of Salamanca.
‍
- Behind the university are the two Cathedrals of Salamanca, the new and the old. The new one is the tallest cathedral in all of Spain, which accompanies the position of having the second tallest church in the nation, surpassed by the Sagrada Familia. Both cathedrals are located together, and with a difference of 4 centuries, the New one began to be built in the 16th century due to the growth of the population due to the University, because the Old cathedral was beginning to become too small. Although in the end it was decided to leave both, being able to make a comparison of the Gothic style of the New and the Romanesque of the Old.
